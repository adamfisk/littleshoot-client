LittleShoot Public Release Candidate
P2P Meets Web 2.0

The LittleShoot team is proud to announce the immediate availability of the first LittleShoot Public Release Candidate. Thanks so much to everyone in the beta group for your many suggestions - we couldn't have done it without you!

We hope this version shows we're listening to your feedback. The major new features include:
  1. Multiple simultaneous searches
  2. Faster downloads
  3. Faster publishing
  4. Publishing progress bars -- useful for publishing really large files
  5. Better spam protection for LimeWire results
  6. Search preferences retention across launches
  7. Spanking new Google App Engine architecture
  8. The LittleShoot Platform

Download LittleShoot

The last feature -- the release of the LittleShoot Platform -- means any site can now add simple JavaScript to send downloads through LittleShoot (such as Flash videos) instead of incurring all the bandwidth costs themselves. We'll be adding documentation for this soon. You can view a more detailed list of the over 50 new changes here.

Several people have also requested the ability to have LittleShoot store central copies of your published files. We weren't able to squeeze that in to this release, but it's coming!

Finally, we're ecstatic to announce the new LittleShoot Advisory Board. Members of the board have made significant contributions to this release, with a particular thanks to Chris Holmes for keeping us on track and to Rachel Johnson, Tim Ferriss, and Douglas Price for their invaluable advice (even though they're not technically on the board!).

The advisory board members include:
  • Ian Clarke - FreeNet, Revver, Sense Array, and Uprizer Labs
  • Chris Holmes - The Open Planning Project, GeoServer, and OpenGeo
  • Rimmy Malhotra - Gratio Capital, Do Well and Do Good
  • Jared Rosoff - Tazz Networks, Media Exchange Research
As always, LittleShoot has no spyware and goes to great lengths to protect your security and privacy.

-Adam Fisk and the LittleShoot Team
