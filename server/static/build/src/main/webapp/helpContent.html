How do I search?

 To search for a file, you simply enter a search term in the search field and either press "Enter" or click the "Search" button. If you want to limit the search to specific media types, you can select the specific media types you want in the check boxes.


I'm not finding what I want. Why?

 LittleShoot is, well, little. While we hope someday we'll grow up to be big, there's not that much content out there while the network is still small. We're a LittleShoot with big dreams.


My friend published a file, but I don't see it. Why?

 LittleShoot only shows the files available from users currently on the network. If your friend turned off his or her computer and they were the only source for that file, it will not be available until he or she comes back online.


How do I download?

 To download a file, you simply click on its link in search results. This is just like clicking on any other link on any other web page, although behind the scenes the LittleShoot plugin is working its magic and downloading different parts of the file from every available user on the network.





Can I save the files I download?

 LittleShoot by default saves files to your Shared folder in your home directory on your computer, so you can always get it from there. With file types the browser doesn't display directly, you can also choose where to download to from the browser window.


How do I publish a file?

 To publish, select the "Browse..." button from either the home page or the "Publish" page. Then select a file to publish from the dialog that appears.

Once you've selected a file, you will then be prompted to add "tags" to the file. Tags are descriptive words describing your file. When other users search, your file will come up if their search term matches one of your tags. Once you publish the file, other users on the network can immediately search for it and download it.


What are tags?

 Tags are other words people can use to find your file. If you're publishing a photo of yourself kayaking in Guatemala, for example, you might add the tags "kayak" and "Guatemala". Even if your photo is called "Me and Billy.jpg", people searching for "kayak" will still see it in their search results.


Do I have to upload the file?

 No, your files always stay on your computer. When other users download them, they get them from your computer and themselves become another source for the file.


Where can I get more help with LittleShoot?

 The LittleShoot forum is a great place to ask questions and interact with other LittleShooters.


LittleShoot always runs in the background on my computer. Why?

 LittleShoot runs in the background to make the files you publish always available. In addition, you become a source for the files you download. LittleShoot is a cooperative system. Each user contributes a little bandwidth serving their files. Everyone contributing a little bit of bandwidth quickly adds up to a lot of bandwidth for the whole LittleShoot network, though, so everyone contributing a little allows everyone to enjoy a network that performs well when you go to download files yourself. The LittleShoot network united together creates an optimized system for distributing your files and your friends' files cheaply and easily.


 Is LittleShoot legal?

 The short answer is yes. The long answer is more involved. First and foremost, LittleShoot is not a tool for distributing copyrighted content. If you don't own the copyright to a file or are unsure of the copyright restrictions on a file, you shouldn't publish it, and you shouldn't download it. LittleShoot adheres to copyright law and something you may have heard of called the "Digital Millennium Copyright Act," or the "DMCA." Under the DMCA, a copyright holder can ask us to take down links to files they own the copyright to. When we receive valid takedown notices, we will remove the content in question. Also, if we see a file on the network we know our users don't own the copyright to, we'll remove it from the system as soon as we're aware of it. Finally, we just don't think that's a particularly interesting use for the technology. We see LittleShoot as a tool to give you better access to the knowledge of the world.
