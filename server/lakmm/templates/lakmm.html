Introduction to Animation
Students will learn to animate hand-drawn, cell, cut out, sand, paint on glass, and puppets. Each week, students will explore a new medium and will animate a short film with sound. At the end of the semester, they will choose their favorite medium and will write, direct, animate, and edit a longer film of their own creation. No experience necessary.

Spring Sessions
Students will make both live-action and animated films in these hit-the-ground-running intensive Winter courses. Topics will include puppet animation, paint on glass, cut out, pixilation, storyboarding, final draft, thumbnails, editing, sound effects, armature building, and set building.

Teacher Biography
Rachel Johnson is the founder of LA Kids Make Movies. She recently relocated to LA after having lived in New York for six years, where she was a professor at NYU, Parson's School of Design, and Pratt Institute. She is currently teaching animation and film at USC. Rachel is an award-winning director of animation, and her film "The Toll Collector" was met with international critical acclaim and won a Golden Hugo at the Chicago Film Festival. She will be releasing her first live action short this Winter.
